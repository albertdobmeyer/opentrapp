# Code of Conduct

## Our intent

We want this project to be a welcoming place for everyone who wants to contribute, regardless of experience level, background, identity, or how much time they can give. The goal of this document is to set out the kind of collaboration we hope to see, and to give people a clear path to flag concerns when something doesn't feel right.

This document is informed by version 2.1 of the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/), adapted to a calmer, more invitational register.

## What we hope for

- **Kindness as a baseline.** Disagreement is fine; condescension is not. When you push back on a contribution, push back on the idea, not the person.
- **Patience with newcomers.** Everyone was new once. A first-time contributor who is learning the codebase deserves a careful, generous review.
- **Clarity about scope.** If a contribution is out of scope, say so kindly and explain why. "Out of scope" is not a judgement on the contributor.
- **Curiosity over certainty.** When you encounter a design choice you do not understand, prefer "help me understand why X" to "X is wrong."
- **Discretion in public.** Disagreements about code happen in pull-request comments. Disagreements about people happen in private — see *Reporting* below.

## What's out of place here

We ask contributors to refrain from conduct that makes other contributors feel unwelcome or unsafe. The Contributor Covenant 2.1 enumerates this in detail; the short version, in our own words:

- Personal attacks, dismissive language, or sustained criticism aimed at a person rather than their work
- Conduct that makes the project unsafe for a contributor on the basis of who they are
- Sharing private information of another contributor without their consent
- Conduct that would clearly be inappropriate in a professional setting

If you are unsure whether something falls in this category, the kindest thing to do is to pause and ask the maintainer privately before continuing.

## Where this applies

This document applies in every space the project occupies: GitHub Issues and Discussions, pull-request reviews, the project's social-media presence, and any in-person or video meeting held under the project's name. It also applies when an individual is representing the project in a public space — speaking at a meet-up, writing a blog post under the project's banner, or replying to questions in a Discord or Slack on the project's behalf.

## Reporting

If you experience or observe conduct that doesn't fit this document, please contact the maintainer privately:

**Email:** albertdobmeyer@proton.me

You can also use this address for any concern that doesn't seem to fit into a public issue — including concerns about a maintainer decision, a pull-request review tone, or a piece of project communication that landed badly.

Reports are read by the maintainer in confidence. The maintainer will acknowledge receipt within 48 hours and will follow up with whatever next step is appropriate to the situation. Reporters are not identified to the subject of a report without the reporter's consent, except where required by applicable law.

## Enforcement

The maintainer is responsible for clarifying expectations, addressing concerns reported under this document, and taking corrective action when the situation calls for it. Corrective action is proportional and is communicated privately to the parties involved. Possibilities include:

- A private clarification of the expectation that was missed
- A request to step back from a particular discussion or pull request
- A temporary or sustained pause on the contributor's participation
- Reverting or removing a contribution that was made in violation of this document

The maintainer commits to applying this document consistently regardless of the contributor's seniority, history, or relationship with the project.

## A note on tone

Software-collaboration norms vary widely by community, by language, and by individual. A phrase that is friendly in one tradition can come across as curt in another. If a piece of communication from another contributor reads more harshly than you suspect was intended, please consider asking for clarification rather than escalating; if a piece of communication of *yours* receives an unexpected reaction, please consider that the reader may be operating from a different baseline than you and respond with the same kindness.

This is a small project. We will try to make it a place that is good to spend time in.
